Organ Donation Awareness

Your contribution can save lives. Learn how you can make a difference by registering as an organ donor.

What is Organ Donation? Organ donation is the process of giving an organ or tissue to help someone who needs a transplant. One donor can save up to 8 lives.

Who Can Donate? Anyone, regardless of age, can become a donor. Medical professionals determine eligibility based on medical history.

How Does It Work? After a person passes away or chooses to donate a kidney or part of the liver while alive, their organs are transplanted into patients in need.

Why Is Organ Donation Important? Thousands of people wait for life-saving transplants every year. Organ donation gives them a second chance at life.

Steps to Become an Organ Donor:

  • Register with an official organ donation organization.
  • Inform your family about your decision.
  • Carry an organ donor card.
  • Keep your medical records updated.
